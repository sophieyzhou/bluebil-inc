The Science

Multiwavelength scleral
reflectance spectroscopy

BlueBil exploits a simple truth: bilirubin absorbs blue light. By actively measuring that absorption in the ocular sclera — and correcting for hemoglobin and distance — we achieve robust, skin-tone-independent bilirubin quantification.

Sensing Architecture Validation Data

Why the Sclera

A consistent optical medium, regardless of race

Melanin, the pigment responsible for skin tone variation, is absent from the scleral tissue. Bilirubin deposits uniformly in the sclera as serum levels rise — creating a predictable, measurable optical signal that works identically across all patients.

Beer-Lambert Law — Foundation of BlueBil
A = log(I₀ / I)
A — Absorbance
I₀ — Reference intensity (white phantom calibration)
I — Measured intensity from sclera

Three-Channel Optical Architecture

Each wavelength plays a distinct role

Primary Channel
470nm

Blue LED — Bilirubin

Bilirubin absorbs maximally near 470 nm. The blue channel is the primary signal — absorbance at this wavelength is the core measure of scleral bilirubin concentration. LED current limited to ≤0.1 mW/cm² for ocular safety.

Reference Channel
530nm

Green LED — Hemoglobin Correction

Hemoglobin absorbs strongly near 540 nm, overlapping with the blue bilirubin channel. The green channel captures this vascular contribution, enabling differential correction that isolates the bilirubin-specific signal.

Distance Verification
850nm

IR LED — Pathlength Control

IR reflectance is insensitive to tissue pigmentation but highly sensitive to probe-to-tissue distance. Two IR checks bracket every measurement, validating consistent optical pathlength. Exposure kept below 140 mW/cm².

Ratiometric Estimation Model
[Bil] = α · (Ablue / Agreen) + β · VIR + γ
α, β, γ — Calibration coefficients
Ablue/Agreen — Isolates bilirubin from hemoglobin background
VIR — Corrects for pathlength variation

Firmware

Six-stage measurement state machine

The ESP32-WROOM-32 runs a deterministic state machine triggered by a physical trigger button. Every measurement is bracketed by placement validation — a failed IR check returns to idle and prompts repositioning, never silently producing a bad reading.

The firmware is organized into modular files: config.h, sensors.cpp, algorithm.cpp, display.cpp, and measurement.cpp.

Known Limitations

What we know, and what comes next

Scientific honesty is core to our approach. The current phantom models are simplified — we acknowledge three key gaps and our path to addressing them.

Light Scattering

Silicone rubber lacks the fibrous collagen microstructure of real scleral tissue, which affects how light scatters and penetrates. Next step: TiO₂-doped scattering phantoms to better model scleral optics.

Hemoglobin Interference

Phantoms contain no vascular component, so hemoglobin's spectral contribution near 540 nm has not yet been incorporated into calibration. Next step: red-dye vascularity phantoms + dual-wavelength model refinement.

Scleral Thickness Variation

Scleral thickness varies across the neonatal population and influences optical pathlength. Homogeneous phantoms cannot capture inter-subject variability. Next step: clinical pilot with paired TSB-BlueBil measurements.

Regulatory Pathway

510(k) clearance via Dräger JM-105 predicate

BlueBil is intended as a non-invasive adjunctive screening tool — not an initial replacement for TSB confirmation. We are pursuing FDA Class II clearance via 510(k) premarket notification.

Predicate Device

Dräger JM-105 (cleared under IYO as Class II). Substantially equivalent intended use — non-invasive bilirubin estimation in neonates. Our scleral site strengthens rather than complicates the equivalence argument.

Photobiological Safety

IEC 62471 compliance required for eye-directed LED system. All exposures maintained well below established thresholds: blue ≤0.1 mW/cm², IR ≤140 mW/cm².

Analytical Performance

510(k) will require accuracy vs. TSB across a diverse neonatal population. Clinical pilot planned with domestic and international NICU partners including Komfo Anokye Teaching Hospital, Ghana.

Q-Sub Meeting Planned

Given the ocular measurement site, we plan a pre-submission meeting with FDA's ophthalmic devices division following completion of our clinical pilot study to clarify review pathway.

Reimbursement Path

TSB billed under CPT 82247 at ~$10–15 (Medicare). TcB already reimbursed in NICUs. BlueBil targets integration into existing DRG bundles, with future CPT code development post-clearance.

IP & Patentability

Engaged University of Michigan Innovation Partnerships for formal prior art search. The combination of three-wavelength active scleral probe with handheld pathlength-corrected form factor appears novel over existing prior art.
