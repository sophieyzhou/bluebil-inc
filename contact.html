Transcutaneous bilirubinometers (TcB) measure bilirubin through skin reflectance, where melanin and subcutaneous fat systematically bias readings — causing documented underperformance in darker-skinned neonates. BlueBil shifts the measurement site entirely to the ocular sclera, which contains no melanin, delivering consistent accuracy across all demographics. BlueBil also operates during phototherapy, which disrupts TcB readings.

All BlueBil LED channels are maintained well below IEC 62471 photobiological safety limits: blue LED ≤0.1 mW/cm², IR LED ≤140 mW/cm². The device is also non-contact — it never touches the eye surface. Measurement occurs at a fixed standoff distance verified by the IR placement system.
